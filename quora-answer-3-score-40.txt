Great question, and one I heard constantly during my years in HR. The short answer: No, most reputable companies do not use a single "ATS score" to auto-reject candidates. Here's what actually happens.

I led recruiting at a Fortune 500 company and later built an ATS tool, so I know both sides. The "ATS score" you see on websites like Jobscan or ResumeWorded is NOT the same thing employers use. Those tools compare your resume against a job description and give you a match percentage. Real enterprise ATS platforms like Workday, Greenhouse, and Lever don't have a magic "score" that filters you out at 40. Instead, they parse your resume into structured data fields (name, experience, skills, education) and let recruiters search and filter.

What does get you filtered? Poor formatting that breaks parsing — if your name doesn't extract correctly or your work history is jumbled, a recruiter may never see you. Also, the absence of key required qualifications (like "CPA" or "5+ years of SaaS sales") means you won't appear in search results.

So that "40% score" you got from an online checker is a helpful diagnostic, not a death sentence. Use it to identify gaps in keyword alignment and formatting.
