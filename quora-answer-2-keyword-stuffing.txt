As someone who spent 12 years in HR and later built an ATS from scratch, I can give you a definitive answer: keyword stuffing hurts more than it helps. Here's why.

Modern ATS platforms are smarter than most job seekers realize. They don't just count keyword frequency — they analyze context. If you list "Python" 15 times across your resume, the parser sees that as unnatural and may flag your resume for keyword manipulation. Worse, when a recruiter opens your parsed profile, they'll see a messy, repetitive document.

Instead, use a targeted approach. Identify 8-10 core skills and requirements from the job description. Weave them naturally into your bullet points under specific roles. For example, instead of listing "project management" five times in a skills section, write: "Led cross-functional project management for a $2M product launch, coordinating with engineering, marketing, and sales." That demonstrates the skill in context.

The real trick is balancing keyword density with readability. Your resume needs to score well with the machine AND impress the human.
