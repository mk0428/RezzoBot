Having spent over a decade in HR and later building an ATS parsing engine myself, I can tell you that "passing ATS" is less about gaming the system and more about speaking its language. Here's what actually works.

First, use a simple, single-column layout. Avoid tables, columns, text boxes, headers/footers, or graphics — ATS parsers choke on these. Stick to standard section headings like "Experience," "Education," and "Skills." Use a clean font like Arial or Calibri at 10-12pt.

Second, keyword matching is critical but misunderstood. Don't just stuff in random industry terms. Study the job description and weave in the exact phrases they use — especially for required skills, certifications, and tools.

Third, use standard date formats (e.g., "Jun 2020 – Aug 2023") and list your experience in reverse chronological order. Save your resume as a .docx file — surprisingly, most ATS systems parse Word documents more reliably than PDFs.
